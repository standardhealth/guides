Overview of vital

The vital signs domain (vital) contains observations for common vital signs, and a panel including all vitals.

Element Description
BloodPressure

Records blood pressure measurements, defined as the force of circulating blood on the walls of the arteries.

Conformance note: The blood pressure profile is based on the FHIR profile for blood pressure, and adds a set of optional elements that can help clinicians interpret the result. Because these additional elements are optional, any BloodPressure that conforms to the current profile also will conform to the FHIR profile, and vice versa.
BloodPressureCuffSize

The size of cuff used, if applicable.
BodyHeight

Records the body height, defined as the distance from the sole to the crown of the head.
BodyMassIndex

A general indicator of the body fat an individual is carrying based upon the ratio of weight to height.
BodyPosition

The position or physical attitude of the body.
BodyTemperature

The measure of the level of heat in the body.
BodyWeight

Records the body weight, defined as the mass or quantity of heaviness of the individual.
DiastolicPressure

The blood pressure after the contraction of the heart while the chambers of the heart refill with blood, when the pressure is lowest.
HeadCircumference

Circumference of the head (typically 0-2 years).
HeartRate

The number of times the heart ventricles contract per unit of time, usually per minute.
OxygenSaturation

Oxygen saturation in Arterial blood by Pulse oximetry.
PreconditionCode

A coded description of the conditions or context of an observation, for example, under sedation, fasting or post-exercise. A qualifier cannot modify the measurement type; for example, a fasting blood sugar is still a blood sugar.
RespiratoryRate

The rate of breathing (inhalation and exhalation) measured within in a unit time, expressed as breaths per minute.
SupplementalOxygenConcentration

Percentage of oxygen delivered to subject during oxygen therapy
SupplementalOxygenFlowrate

Oxygen gas flow rate delivered to subject during oxygen therapy
SystolicPressure

The blood pressure during the contraction of the left ventricle of the heart, when blood pressure is at its highest.
VitalSign

Abstract class parent for all vital sign profiles.
VitalSignsPanel

A summary of vital signs, including weight, height (or length), blood pressure, respiration rate, oxygen saturation, heart rate, and body temperature, BMI, and (optionally) head circumference. Each member of the panel is treated as a separate, independent observation. The panel is a grouping for convenience so all vitals can be retrieved using one LOINC code (85353-1).
